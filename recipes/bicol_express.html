Bicol Express

Description

Bicol Express is a spicy Filipino dish made with pork, shrimp paste, and coconut milk. It originates from the Bicol region of the Philippines,
known for its love of spicy food. The dish is characterized by its rich and creamy sauce, which is infused with the heat of chili peppers. It's
typically served with steamed rice and is a favorite among those who enjoy bold flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 4-5 long green chili peppers (siling pangsigang)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and ginger. Saut√© until fragrant and the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the pork belly pieces and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for a few minutes until the pork is well coated.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 20-30 minutes, or until the pork is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Add the long green chili peppers and season with salt, pepper, and fish sauce (if using). Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Notes

Feel free to adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or fewer chili peppers. You can also use other cuts of pork, such as pork shoulder or pork belly, depending on your preference. Bicol Express is best enjoyed with a side of steamed rice to balance the rich and spicy flavors of the dish.

Variations

Some variations of Bicol Express include adding vegetables like eggplant or string beans, or using seafood like shrimp or fish instead of pork. You can also experiment with different types of chili peppers to suit your taste. Additionally, some recipes may include a splash of vinegar for added tanginess or a hint of sweetness with the addition of sugar.

Serving Suggestions

Bicol Express is typically served as a main dish alongside steamed rice. It pairs well with a side of fresh vegetables or a simple salad to balance the richness of the dish. You can also serve it with a dipping sauce made of vinegar, soy sauce, and chopped chili peppers for an extra kick. For a complete meal, consider serving it with other Filipino dishes like sinigang (sour soup) or lumpia (spring rolls).
