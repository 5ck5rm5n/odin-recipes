Lechon Recipes

Description

Lechon is a traditional Filipino dish that features a whole roasted pig, known for its crispy skin and tender meat. It is often served during special occasions and celebrations, making it a centerpiece of Filipino feasts. The pig is marinated with a blend of spices and herbs, then cooked over an open flame or in an oven until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Lechon is typically served with a dipping sauce made from liver sauce or vinegar, and it is enjoyed with steamed rice. The dish is not only a culinary delight but also a symbol of Filipino hospitality and culture.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole pig (about 20-30 kg)
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 1 cup garlic, minced
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1 cup lemongrass, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Banana leaves (for wrapping)
  • Cooking oil (for basting)

Instructions

  1. Clean the pig thoroughly and remove any hair. Pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the soy sauce, vinegar, calamansi juice, minced garlic, chopped onion, chopped lemongrass, black peppercorns, salt, and sugar to create a marinade.
  3. Rub the marinade all over the pig, making sure to get it into the cavity and under the skin. Let it marinate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  4. Preheat the oven or prepare a charcoal pit for roasting. If using an oven, preheat it to 180째C (350째F).
  5. Wrap the pig in banana leaves to keep it moist during cooking. If using a charcoal pit, place the pig on a spit and secure it properly.
  6. Roast the pig for about 4-6 hours, basting it with cooking oil every 30 minutes to achieve a crispy skin. If using an oven, place the wrapped pig on a roasting rack and cook for the same amount of time, turning it occasionally for even cooking.
  7. Check the internal temperature of the pig; it should reach at least 75째C (165째F) to ensure it is fully cooked.
  8. Once done, remove the pig from the oven or pit and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes before carving.
  9. Serve the lechon with liver sauce or vinegar on the side, along with steamed rice and your favorite side dishes.
