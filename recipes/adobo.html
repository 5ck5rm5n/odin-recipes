Adobo Recipes

Description

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish made with meat, usually chicken or pork, marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, then simmered until tender. It's known for its savory and tangy flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken or pork, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine vinegar, soy sauce, minced garlic, bay leaves, black peppercorns, and sugar (if using). Mix well.
  2. Add the chicken or pork pieces to the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for better flavor.
  3. Heat cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Remove the meat from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and brown the meat on all sides.
  4. Pour the reserved marinade into the pot with the browned meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and cover. Simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the meat is tender.
  5. Adjust seasoning with salt if needed. If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can remove the lid and let it simmer uncovered for a few more minutes.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.
