Pancit Recipes

Description

Pancit is a traditional Filipino noodle dish that comes in various forms and flavors. It is often served during celebrations and special occasions, symbolizing long life and good fortune. Pancit can be made with different types of noodles, such as rice noodles or egg noodles, and is typically stir-fried with a mix of vegetables, meat, and seafood. The dish is known for its savory and slightly sweet flavor, often enhanced with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and a hint of citrus. Pancit is not only a delicious meal but also a cultural staple in Filipino cuisine, enjoyed by people of all ages.

Ingredients

  • 250g rice noodles (bihon or canton)
  • 200g chicken breast, sliced
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, cabbage)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon or calamansi, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 10-15 minutes until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, and saut√© until fragrant and the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the sliced chicken breast and cook until no longer pink, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add the shrimp and cook until they turn pink and opaque, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until they are tender but still crisp.
  6. Add the soaked rice noodles to the pan, along with the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated and heated through, about 3-5 minutes.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and transfer to a serving platter. Garnish with lemon or calamansi wedges.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Pancit!
